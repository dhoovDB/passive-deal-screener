## 2. Return Stress-Test
**No category baseline applies.** `01` marks office *variable* (post-2020 occupancy reset still working through 2025–26), and `05` deliberately gives office no spread-table row — a forced comparator would be false grounding. The honest benchmark is the deal's own underwriting, and the absence of a stable baseline is itself information: an 18% IRR in a category with no floor is an assertion, not a comparison 🟡.

What can be said from references:
- NPI 2025 office total return was **3.4% — the lowest of the property types** (`05`, unlevered overlay), consistent with a still-dislocated sector.
- An 18% projection therefore embeds a *recovery thesis* — reabsorption, releasing, and an exit buyer — none of which is evidenced in the materials.

Swing assumptions (all undisclosed):
1. **Physical occupancy and sublease overhang** — the entire post-2020 question (`GEN-19`).
2. **The 2-year debt maturity** — refinance or sale into whatever office credit market exists then (`GEN-09`).
3. **Releasing economics** — lease expiration schedule and the capex to compete (move-in-ready vs shell), both absent.

The "steep discount to its 2018 sale" deserves its own caution: **2018 is a pre-reset anchor.** A discount to a price from a structurally different demand regime is not evidence of cheapness — the value-trap `01` warns about is precisely a deal priced as "cheap vs then" rather than underwritten on "cash flows now."

## 10. Verdict
**Pass as presented.** This is the deal type where `01` is bluntest: office with no physical-occupancy or sublease disclosure is **unscreenable** — and this deal adds a 2-year debt clock and a pre-reset price anchor to the missing data. There is a version of 2026 office that works (deep discount, verified physical occupancy, long WALT, funded capex, extended debt), but proving this is that deal requires exactly the disclosures the materials omit. **Re-screen if:** physical occupancy and sublease data arrive and are strong, the lease roll clears the debt maturity, and a credible extension/refi path exists. Until then the 18% is a recovery bet with the evidence withheld. Suits only an LP explicitly making a contrarian office-recovery allocation with capital sized to lose.
