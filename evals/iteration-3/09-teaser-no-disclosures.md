## 2. Return Stress-Test

There is no underwriting to stress; only the headline can be located against the references:

- **If "15%+" is a net IRR** and the sub-strategy is value-add, it sits at the aggressive end of the `01` band (12–18% target; realized clusters 12–15%) 🟡. If the strategy is core/core-plus (8–12% per `01`), 15%+ is *outside* category norms and demands an explanation — but the sub-strategy is unstated, so even that test can't be run.
- **If it's gross**, the fee stack (undisclosed) takes an unknowable bite; the `02` worked example turns a 15% gross into ≈10.5–11% net through a standard stack. "15%+ returns" with no basis could be either number.
- **Benchmark (`05`):** taken at face value as net over a typical 5-year fund lock-up, 15% vs VNQ 6.47% = ≈850bps — comfortably above the ~300–400bps hurdle 🔴 (assumed basis, assumed hold, unverifiable). The comparison is arithmetic on a marketing phrase, and `05`'s dispersion caveat cuts hardest exactly here: the marketed figure is the top-quartile story, and the median fund underperforms it.

## 4. Fee Stack Summary (`02`)

**Nothing disclosed — GEN-16 fires.** Per `02`, a multifamily equity fund normally carries: acquisition 1–2%, asset management 1–2% annual, disposition 0.5–1%, promote 15–30% over a 6–10% pref, admin 0.1–0.5% — plus, if access runs through a feeder/fund-of-funds wrapper, another 50–150bps of annual drag. **Gross-to-net drag is not computable — that is the finding, and with "15%+" of unstated basis, the entire return claim is unpriceable.**

## 10. Verdict

**Pass as presented — insufficient disclosure.** The essential disclosures are not partially absent; they are entirely absent — no sponsor, strategy, structure, fees, financials, or return basis — so there is no core return story to underwrite, only a marketing number ("15%+") whose gross/net basis alone swings the LP outcome by hundreds of bps (`02`). Biggest swing factor: whether a real offering package exists and what "15%+" turns out to mean inside it. **Re-screen list:** PPM with full fee/waterfall detail; sub-strategy and pipeline; sponsor identity with realized net-to-LP track record; leverage strategy and debt terms; distribution schedule and liquidity terms. This teaser is an invitation to start diligence, not evidence a deal exists; treat it as exactly that.
