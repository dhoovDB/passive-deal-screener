## 2. Return Stress-Test

Swing assumptions: **rent growth / renovation premium**, **exit cap (held flat — conservative)**, **expense inflation**. Refi risk is structurally absent (fixed-rate debt outlives the plan).

- **Base.** No target is stated; `01` puts the category at 12–18% target gross-side, realized 12–15% net blended. On an assumed 15% gross (🔴 assumed — the first ask in §8), the disclosed fee stack nets to **≈10.9%** (fee-drag model, 6-yr hold assumed: 412bps/yr total drag — §4).
- **Bear.** A sensitivity is actually provided 🟢 (contents not summarized here — verify it stresses the right three: flat rents, higher exit cap, no refi; Q-DS-03 confirms). Structurally the deal is bear-resistant: flat exit cap means no baked-in compression to give back; fixed-rate debt means no rate-path exposure; maturity two years past exit means a soft 2031-32 market can be waited out rather than sold into.
- **Benchmark (`05`, benchmark_comparator):** est. net ~10.9% vs VNQ 6.47% = **≈440bps premium** against a ~400–600bps hurdle for a 5–7-year lock-up → **clears, lower half of the band** 🟡 on assumed gross. This is the honest read: a clean structure at market fees still only *thinly* out-earns the liquid comparator after drag — which is why the actual target return and the realized-deal IRRs are the make-or-break verification, not the structure.
- **Unlevered overlay (`05`):** NPI ≈4.9% — a low-double-digit net still leans on leverage and value-add execution; with fixed-rate debt and a flat exit cap, that leverage is at least not a rate bet (GEN-07 does not fire).

## 10. Verdict

**Pursue.** Enough is disclosed to underwrite the core return story — this deal affirmatively clears every structural screen the references define, several at better-than-market terms (whole-of-fund + clawback, compounding pref, 10% pari-passu co-invest), and the residual absences are conditions, not the verdict. Biggest swing factor: the actual target return — the structure guarantees the LP a fair *split*, not a good *outcome*, and at market-typical gross (~15%) the estimated ~10.9% net clears the VNQ-plus-illiquidity hurdle only in the lower half of the band. Conditions to close: (1) the target net IRR and pro forma, confirmed to clear ~6.5% + 400–600bps; (2) the five exits verified; (3) submarket rent-growth support; (4) PPM matches the summary. Suits an LP wanting classic value-add exposure with genuinely aligned sponsorship; what must be true is simply that the operational plan (renovate, raise rents) performs — the structure has already removed the financing-engineering ways this category usually fails.
