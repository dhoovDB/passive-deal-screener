## 2. Return Stress-Test

Office is a *variable* class (`01`): **no category return baseline and no spread comparator** (`05` gives it no row) — the benchmark is the deal's own underwriting, and this deal has not shown one. The absent baseline is itself informative.

- **The claimed 18%** cannot be stress-tested: no exit cap, no rent/releasing assumptions, no capex plan, no debt terms beyond a maturity date. What `05`'s unlevered overlay does say: office was the *worst* NPI property type in 2025 (3.4% total return) — an 18% projection sits ~1,450bps above the sector's unlevered reality, so nearly all of it must come from leverage, basis recovery, or releasing execution 🟡 (GEN-07 family — unverifiable without the underwriting).
- **The "discount to 2018 sale" framing** is anchoring, not analysis 🟡: 2018 office pricing reflects a pre-reset demand curve that `01` treats as structurally gone. Cheap against a dead comp is not cheap; the relevant basis test is price vs *current* replacement-cost-adjusted, WFH-adjusted cash flow.
- **85% leased ≠ 85% occupied.** Per `01`, leased space can sit dark; dark space doesn't renew. Physical occupancy and sublease overhang — the two numbers that decide whether 85% leased is durable income or a slow-motion vacancy — are exactly the two numbers the materials omit (GEN-19).
- **The debt wall is the real clock:** maturity in 2 years, hold unstated → the plan must refinance or sell into the 2028 office capital-markets environment, whatever it is. Office refi availability remains the sector's binding constraint 🟡 (GEN-09).

## 3. Where LP Returns Come From

Unstated — but an 18% IRR on a stabilizing office asset cannot plausibly come from in-place cash flow at 85% leased with dark-space risk; it must be driven by basis appreciation (buy low, exit at a recovered price) plus leverage → an exit-and-financing story (GEN-07/GEN-08 family) 🟡 whose two load-bearing assumptions — the exit buyer's cap rate for office in ~2028+ and a refi across the 2-year maturity — are both undisclosed and both outside the sponsor's control. Treated as fired-pending-rebuttal given the return can only plausibly be exit-driven; the decomposition is Q-DS-01.

## 4. Fee Stack Summary (`02`)

## 10. Verdict

**Pass as presented — insufficient disclosure.** Office is a *variable* class whose only valid benchmark is the deal's own underwriting (`01`, `05`) — and the underwriting is not here: physical occupancy, sublease overhang, submarket, lease roll, capex, debt terms, fees, and hold are all absent, which per `01` makes the claimed 18% literally unscreenable. GEN-19 fires on the omission itself, and the 2-year debt maturity (GEN-09) puts a hard clock on a plan nobody has articulated. Biggest swing factor: physical occupancy and the refi path — together they decide whether this is a genuine basis play or a value trap priced off a dead 2018 comp. **Re-screen list:** physical occupancy + sublease overhang + submarket reabsorption data; full rent roll with expirations; complete debt terms and refi/extension plan; capex-to-compete budget; fee stack and waterfall; sponsor's realized office track record. Distressed-basis Class A office *can* work in 2026 — but only for an LP shown exactly the numbers this deal declined to show.
