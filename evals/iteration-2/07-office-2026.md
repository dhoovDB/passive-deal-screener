## 2. Return Stress-Test

Because office is a *variable* class, there is no category IRR band and no forced public comparator — the benchmark is the deal's **own underwriting**, and this deal discloses almost none of it. What can be said:

Swing assumptions: **(a) physical occupancy vs the 85% leased figure** — leased space can sit dark (`01`); dark space doesn't renew; **(b) the 2-year debt maturity** — the entire plan must survive a refinance or sale event in ~2026–2028 office credit markets; **(c) submarket reabsorption and sublease overhang** — undisclosed, and it determines whether today's discount is an entry point or a value trap.

- **Base:** 18% as claimed — unverifiable; no NOI, no basis figure, no exit assumption disclosed 🔴.
- **Bull:** resilient submarket, high physical occupancy, WFH-stable tenancy, lender-friendly refi → the discounted basis produces equity-like upside; this is the case the marketing implies.
- **Bear:** 85% leased conceals (say) 50–60% physical occupancy; expirations roll into a submarket with sublease shadow supply; the loan matures in year 2 into an office-hostile credit market → forced sale at a wider cap or a capital call. The 2018 price is irrelevant to this outcome — "cheap vs 2018" is exactly how office value-traps are marketed post-reset (`01`: "pricing creates both opportunity and value-trap") 🟡.
- **Benchmark note (`05`):** no spread row for office by design; forcing VNQ here would be false grounding. The NPI overlay is still informative: office was the **worst NPI property type in 2025 at 3.4% unlevered** — an 18% levered claim sits ~14–15 points above the sector's unlevered reality, so the return is carried by leverage + basis recovery, not in-place income (`GEN-07` probe) 🟡 → `Q-DS-02`.

## 3. Where LP Returns Come From

An 18% IRR on a discounted-basis office play is a **basis-recovery-and-exit story**: buy below replacement/2018, hope occupancy and pricing normalize, sell or refi. In-place income at 85% leased (physical unknown) cannot plausibly carry 18% when unlevered sector income runs ~3–4% (`05` NPI) — so the return is predominantly exit/leverage-driven → `GEN-08` (🟡 inferred; not decomposable from disclosure — demand the split) with `GEN-07` probe pending debt terms → `Q-DS-01`, `Q-DS-02`.

## 9. Diligence Checklist

- Rent roll + lease abstracts: expirations vs the 2-year debt maturity, credit of major tenants.
- Independent physical-occupancy evidence (badge/utilization data, site visit) — do not accept leased % as a proxy.
- Submarket report from a third-party brokerage: direct + sublease availability, reabsorption trend.
- Debt documents: maturity, extensions, cash-flow sweep triggers, guaranties.
- Appraisal on current income, plus replacement-cost analysis — test the "discount" against 2026 anchors, not 2018.
- PPM/fee schedule; sponsor background/regulatory search; office-specific track record verification.

## 10. Verdict

**Pass as presented — insufficient disclosure.** `01` deems office unscreenable without class, market, and WFH-mix disclosure — this deal supplies only class. The materials' silence on physical occupancy and sublease space is not a gap, it is `GEN-19` firing (RED): 2026 office underwriting that doesn't engage the reset is using a baseline that no longer exists. Biggest swing factor: **physical occupancy against the 2-year debt maturity** — those two numbers together decide whether the discount is an entry or a trap.

**Re-screen list:** physical occupancy; sublease overhang + submarket reabsorption; lease-expiration schedule; full debt terms and extension options; hold period; purchase price and underwriting model; capex/TI budget; fee stack; sponsor's post-2020 office record; co-invest. **What would have to be true to pursue:** physical occupancy near the leased figure, expirations light inside the debt term, a maturity with real extension options, and a basis that pencils against *current* NOI — not against 2018. **Who it suits:** only an LP explicitly underwriting distressed-office recovery risk with capital they can afford to see called or locked through a workout.
